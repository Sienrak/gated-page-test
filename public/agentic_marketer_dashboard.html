The Race to the Top: How Agentic Marketers Turn AI Productivity Into Brand Differentiation

For marketing leaders, the past year has felt like living in a pressure cooker. Between board expectations for instant AI transformation, the relentless pace of new tool launches, and the nagging worry that everyone using the same technology will make your brand sound generic, it's no wonder many CMOs are experiencing AI anxiety rather than AI excitement.

"That's not strategy," says Diego Lomanto, Chief Marketing Officer at Writer. "It's a race to the bottom."

But there's another path forward, one that leading marketing organizations at companies like KPMG, American Eagle, and Qualcomm are already proving works. It's called the agentic marketing engine, and it represents a fundamental shift in how we think about AI's role in modern marketing.

The Strategic Choice Every CMO Faces

The marketing world stands at a crossroads. One path leads to a future flooded with derivative, reverse-engineered content that erodes customer trust and leads to long-term brand decay. The other path uses AI not as a replacement for creativity, but as a catalyst for it.

"The real question is no longer about what AI can do for us," Lomanto explains, "but what we as marketers are prepared to do with the freedom that it provides."

This isn't just philosophical posturing. Companies are already seeing the results of choosing one path over the other. The difference comes down to a simple but powerful framework: use AI to boost productivity, then funnel those gains into what truly sets you apart.

The Five-Pillar Marketing Engine

The agentic marketing engine operates on five interconnected pillars. The first two, speed and scale, drive productivity gains. The final three, humanity, high touch, and relevance, create brand differentiation. Together, they form a system that helps marketing organizations move faster while standing out more distinctly.

Speed: Closing the Gap Between Insight and Action

In today's market, speed has moved from nice-to-have to competitive necessity. An AI agent can take a single research report and automatically execute a dozen downstream tasks, from drafting summaries to creating social posts to updating sales decks.

Consider this real-world example from KPMG: "One of my stakeholders reached out to me on a Wednesday morning and he was tasked to write an article for a publication," recalls a KPMG team member. "I was able to send him a draft of all of the answers to his questions within an hour. He thought it would take me all day long, which it probably would have had I not had Writer."

That's the power of speed through AI. A task that could have consumed an entire workday now takes an hour, freeing up seven hours for higher-value strategic work.

Scale: From Six Photos to 500 Content Pieces

The volume demands of modern marketing would be impossible without AI assistance. As one American Eagle executive put it: "When I was at Calvin Klein, a campaign meant six beautiful pictures, a Soho billboard, a double page spread in Vogue, high five, and you move on to the next. Now at American Eagle, we're producing at least five hundred pieces of content a week."

But it's not just about producing more content. With 34,000 SKUs on their website, American Eagle needed a way to handle product descriptions at scale while maintaining brand voice. The solution wasn't hiring hundreds of writers; it was using AI agents to replicate excellence across every touchpoint.

Humanity: Empowering Creative People

Here's where the equation shifts from productivity to differentiation. "In an AI world, we actually need more human creativity, not less," Lomanto emphasizes. "We need the rule breakers, the storytellers."

By taking on the 80% of work that's repetitive and tactical, AI frees up your most valuable resource: your team's creative energy. This allows them to focus on the 20% that is strategic and inspired.

"Too many companies treat AI like just another tool for efficiency or for cost cutting. But if you don't see AI as a disruptor, you're really missing the true potential of it." - KPMG

The proof? "Thousands of hours saved" at KPMG, reinvested in providing more value to partners and clients. At Qualcomm, workload reductions of 30-40% translated into "happier employees" who are "more productive, more creative, and more satisfied."

High Touch: Deepening Customer Relationships

When your team has more time, what's the highest-value activity they can do? Build relationships. Whether you're B2B or B2C, creating deeper, more personal connections with customers matters more than ever.

At Writer, productivity gains from speed and scale get reinvested heavily in field marketing and in-person events. Why? Because AI makes it possible to maintain high-touch human connection at scale. Field marketers use agents to create entire enablement kits for events, but the events themselves remain focused on genuine human interaction.

Relevance: Maintaining Your Unique Voice

Every interaction needs to be meaningful, with a consistent brand voice showing up on the right channels with the right message. How do AI agents help ensure relevance? By building on your company's unique data and brand rules.

At Qualcomm, the team uploaded approximately 1,200 trademark and other terms into Writer, providing automatic guidance to marketing teams creating assets. The result? "The assets and materials when they come through tend to be more compliant from the get go," allowing the review team to operate more efficiently while maintaining brand standards.

The 1x to 10x Transformation

Most marketing teams are stuck in two modes. In the "1x world," they create great assets but then face a slow, painful process of manually translating them for other teams. By the time sales gets the asset, the market conversation has moved on.

Some teams have graduated to the "5x world," armed with generative AI tools. Things are faster, but teams now spend their days as human APIs, copying and pasting between five different tools. The process remains fragmented, with no governance, and strategic intent gets lost with every new prompt.

The "10x world" operates differently. As Laura Weaver, Writer's Director of Enterprise Content, explains: "At the moment, I'm orchestrating half a dozen major strategic initiatives. Before Writer, I could probably only keep up with one, maybe two of these at a time."

The shift requires three strategic questions:

  1. When starting something new: "How can we design this AI-native from day one?"
  2. When fixing a broken process: "Could an agent run this better and free humans for strategic work?"
  3. When considering new vendors: "Is this a tool purchase or an AI agent opportunity?"

From Personal Productivity to Organizational Capability

The real breakthrough comes when individual workflows become organizational assets through playbooks. "The mastery isn't locked in one person's head," Weaver notes. "It's a scalable asset for the entire organization."

Anyone on the team can execute complex workflows with power-user rigor instantly. Demand gen teams can monitor campaigns and ideate on ad copy in a click. Product marketing teams can conduct deep competitive research and pull market data. Sales teams can automate follow-ups while checking LinkedIn for lead details, all in time for their next call.

The Path Forward

"The antidote to fear isn't just more tools, it's a new mindset," Lomanto concludes. "It's the shift from being a passive user to an active architect."

This transformation requires excellent technology built for enterprise reliability and consistency at scale. But more importantly, it requires visionary leadership from people willing to rebuild how work gets done.

"Brand will become the most valuable competitive edge a company can have," Lomanto predicts. "Standing out from the noise will be crucial. And the only way to do that is by focusing on what makes you you and by building genuine connections with your customers."

The question isn't whether your organization will adopt AI for marketing. The question is whether you'll use it to race to the bottom or race to the top.
